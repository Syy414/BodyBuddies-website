Leg Cramps

Category Details
Causes
  • Sitting for long periods of time
  • Overusing your muscles
  • Standing or working on concrete floors
  • Having poor posture during the day
  • Kidney failure, diabetic nerve damage, mineral deficits, and issues with blood flow
Treatment
  • Stretch
  • Massage
  • Stand
  • Walk
  • Apply heat
  • Apply cold
  • Take pain medications
  • Elevate the affected area
Specific Techniques
  • Cramp in the Foot: Stand with your weight on the front of the foot. Flex your toes upward.
  • Cramp in the Calf Muscle: Extend the affected leg straight out, and flex the foot upward towards your body.
Prevention
  • Stay hydrated
  • Stretch your muscles
  • Use proper footwear
  • Avoid overexertion
  • Consider dietary adjustments

Neck Spasms

Category Details
Causes
  • Poor posture
  • Muscle strain or overuse
  • Injuries
  • Stress and tension
  • Dehydration
  • Cradling a cellphone using your neck
  • Craning your neck to stare at a computer, TV, or phone screen for too long
  • Carrying a heavy bag over one shoulder
  • Straining your neck muscles during exercise
  • Clenching or grinding your teeth while asleep
  • Carrying heavy objects, especially using one arm
  • Holding too much tension in your neck due to stress
  • Sleeping in a position that prevents head and body alignment
  • Using a pillow that doesn‚Äôt provide enough neck support
Treatment
  • Heat therapy
  • Stretching exercises
  • Hydration
  • Massage
  • Pain medications
  • Stretch your neck
  • Massage your neck muscles
  • Get active
  • Address stress
  • Sleep with your head and body in alignment
  • Try cold/heat therapy
Prevention
  • Maintain good posture
  • Take breaks during long periods of sitting
  • Stay hydrated
  • Practice stress management techniques
  • Perform neck stretches regularly to keep muscles loose and build strength
  • Incorporate stretching into your exercise routine (yoga, warm-ups, cooldowns)
  • Take periodic stretch breaks at work, especially if sitting in front of a screen
  • Adjust screens and monitors to eye level to avoid straining your neck
  • Sleep on your back or side with a pillow to keep your head and body aligned
  • Use a mouth guard if you grind or clench your teeth at night
  • Hold your phone to your ear or use the speakerphone to go hands-free
  • Avoid carrying heavy objects or bearing weight on a single arm/shoulder
  • Stay hydrated by drinking water throughout the day
